Sprint

Solve Big Problems and Test New Ideas in Just Five Days Pecahkan Masalah Besar dan Uji Ide Baru Hanya dalam Lima Hari

The Concept Konsep Dasar

Why Sprint? Mengapa Sprint?

"The longer it takes to develop an idea, the less likely it is to be successful. Sprint compresses months of work into a single week." "Semakin lama waktu yang dibutuhkan untuk mengembangkan ide, semakin kecil kemungkinannya untuk sukses. Sprint memadatkan pekerjaan berbulan-bulan menjadi satu minggu."

Jake Knapp created the Sprint process at Google Ventures (GV) to solve a critical problem: teams waste too much time debating and building things nobody wants. The Sprint is a "Greatest Hits" of business strategy, behavioral science, and design thinking, packaged into a step-by-step process that any team can use. It is rigorous, fast, and remarkably effective.

Jake Knapp menciptakan proses Sprint di Google Ventures (GV) untuk memecahkan masalah kritis: tim membuang terlalu banyak waktu berdebat dan membangun hal yang tidak diinginkan siapa pun. Sprint adalah kumpulan "Lagu Terbaik" dari strategi bisnis, ilmu perilaku, dan pemikiran desain, dikemas dalam proses langkah-demi-langkah yang bisa digunakan tim mana pun. Proses ini ketat, cepat, dan sangat efektif.

The Rules of the Game

Aturan Main

  • 1. The Decider 1. Sang Pemutus (The Decider)

    Democracy fails in product design. You need one person (usually the CEO or Product Lead) who has the final authority to say "Yes" or "No." The team generates ideas, but the Decider makes the call. This eliminates endless debates.

    Demokrasi gagal dalam desain produk. Anda butuh satu orang (biasanya CEO atau Product Lead) yang punya otoritas akhir untuk bilang "Ya" atau "Tidak." Tim menghasilkan ide, tapi Pemutus yang mengambil keputusan. Ini menghilangkan debat tanpa akhir.

  • 2. No Devices 2. Tanpa Gadget

    Laptops and phones are banned from the Sprint room. They are distraction machines. You need 100% brainpower focused on the problem. There are designated breaks for checking email.

    Laptop dan ponsel dilarang di ruang Sprint. Mereka adalah mesin distraksi. Anda butuh 100% daya otak fokus pada masalah. Ada jam istirahat khusus untuk cek email.

  • 3. Get Real Surface Data 3. Data Permukaan Nyata

    Don't rely on abstract reports. The goal is to build a realistic prototype and test it with *real* humans by Friday. Seeing a customer react to a product beats any internal meeting.

    Jangan andalkan laporan abstrak. Tujuannya adalah membangun prototipe realistis dan mengujinya dengan manusia *nyata* pada hari Jumat. Melihat pelanggan bereaksi terhadap produk mengalahkan rapat internal mana pun.

Monday

Map & Target Peta & Target

Monday is about unloading information and creating a shared brain. You start at the end and work backward.

Senin adalah tentang membongkar informasi dan menciptakan "otak bersama". Anda mulai dari akhir dan bekerja mundur.

1. Start at the End (The Map)

1. Mulai dari Akhir (Peta)

Instead of diving into solutions, you must define the long-term goal. "Where do we want to be in 6 months?"

How to Draw the Map:
On the left side of the whiteboard, list the actors (customers, users). On the right side, list the final goal (purchase, sign-up). In the middle, draw a simple flowchart of how they get there. Keep it simple (5-15 steps max). This map anchors the team for the whole week.

Alih-alih langsung ke solusi, Anda harus mendefinisikan tujuan jangka panjang. "Di mana kita ingin berada dalam 6 bulan?"

Cara Menggambar Peta:
Di sisi kiri papan tulis, daftar aktornya (pelanggan, pengguna). Di sisi kanan, daftar tujuan akhir (pembelian, pendaftaran). Di tengah, gambar diagram alur sederhana bagaimana mereka sampai ke sana. Buat sederhana (maks 5-15 langkah). Peta ini menjadi jangkar tim sepanjang minggu.

2. Ask the Experts & HMW 2. Tanya Ahli & HMW (Bagaimana Jika Kita)

In the afternoon, you interview experts within the company (engineers, sales, customer support).

"How Might We" (HMW) Notes:
While listening, you don't take normal notes. You rephrase problems as opportunities. If an expert says "Users are confused by the menu," you write on a sticky note: "HMW make the menu intuitive?"

This small linguistic shift moves the brain from panic ("Oh no, a problem!") to creativity ("Let's solve this!").

Di sore hari, Anda mewawancarai ahli di perusahaan (insinyur, sales, CS).

Catatan "How Might We" (HMW):
Saat mendengarkan, jangan catat biasa. Ubah masalah jadi peluang. Jika ahli bilang "Pengguna bingung dengan menunya," Anda tulis di sticky note: "HMW (Bagaimana jika kita) membuat menu intuitif?"

Perubahan bahasa kecil ini memindahkan otak dari panik ("Oh tidak, masalah!") ke kreativitas ("Ayo selesaikan ini!").

Case Study: Blue Bottle Coffee Studi Kasus: Blue Bottle Coffee

They wanted to sell coffee online. Their map showed a confusing path for users to choose beans. The HMW notes revealed the core problem: "HMW make buying coffee online feel like talking to a barista?" This guided their entire target for the week.

Mereka ingin jual kopi online. Peta mereka menunjukkan jalur membingungkan bagi pengguna memilih biji kopi. Catatan HMW mengungkap masalah inti: "HMW membuat beli kopi online terasa seperti ngobrol dengan barista?" Ini memandu seluruh target minggu itu.

Tuesday

Sketching Solutions Sketsa Solusi

Tuesday is for solutions. But no group brainstorming. Group brainstorming is loud and favors extroverts, not the best ideas. Instead, everyone sketches alone.

Selasa adalah untuk solusi. Tapi dilarang brainstorming kelompok. Brainstorming kelompok itu berisik dan menguntungkan ekstrovert, bukan ide terbaik. Sebaliknya, semua orang membuat sketsa sendirian.

1. Remix and Improve

1. Remix dan Tingkatkan

Start the morning by looking at existing solutions. Not just competitors, but other industries. If you are building a fitness app, look at how banking apps handle data visualization. This is called "Lightning Demos." It fills the brain with raw material.

Mulai pagi dengan melihat solusi yang ada. Bukan cuma pesaing, tapi industri lain. Jika Anda buat aplikasi kebugaran, lihat bagaimana aplikasi bank menangani visualisasi data. Ini disebut "Demo Kilat." Ini mengisi otak dengan bahan mentah.

2. The Four-Step Sketch

2. Sketsa Empat Langkah

  • Step 1: Notes (20 min) Walk around the room, look at the whiteboard, take notes. Gather your thoughts.
  • Step 2: Ideas (20 min) Doodle rough ideas. No one will see these but you. It's about quantity.
  • Step 3: Crazy 8s (8 min)

    Fold a paper into 8 sections. Sketch 8 variations of your best idea in 8 minutes (1 minute per sketch). This forces you past your first "reasonable" idea and into innovative territory. It's fast and stressful, but effective.

    Lipat kertas jadi 8 bagian. Sketsa 8 variasi dari ide terbaik Anda dalam 8 menit (1 menit per sketsa). Ini memaksa Anda melewati ide "masuk akal" pertama dan masuk ke wilayah inovatif. Cepat dan bikin stres, tapi efektif.

  • Step 4: Solution Sketch (30-90 min)

    Create a fully formed concept. It must be self-explanatory (anonymous). No pitching allowed. The sketch must stand on its own merit.

    Buat konsep utuh. Harus bisa menjelaskan dirinya sendiri (anonim). Dilarang pitching. Sketsa harus berdiri di atas kualitasnya sendiri.

Wednesday

Decide & Storyboard Putuskan & Storyboard

Wednesday morning, you have a stack of anonymous sketches. You can't prototype them all. You must decide.

Rabu pagi, Anda punya tumpukan sketsa anonim. Anda tak bisa memprototipe semuanya. Anda harus memutuskan.

The Sticky Decision

Keputusan Lengket

  1. Art Museum: Tape all sketches to the wall.
  2. Heat Map: Everyone walks around silently with dot stickers. Put dots on parts you like (a headline, a button, a layout).
  3. Speed Critique: The Facilitator talks through each sketch, highlighting the "Heat Map" areas. The author remains silent until the end.
  4. Supervote: The Decider gets special big stickers. Whatever they vote for is what gets built. Period.

The Rumble (Conflict is Good)

The Rumble (Konflik itu Bagus)

If the Decider likes two conflicting ideas (e.g., Idea A is a mobile app, Idea B is a website), don't compromise. Compromises make bad products.

Instead, do a "Rumble." Build BOTH. You will test Prototype A against Prototype B on Friday. Let the best idea win in the real world.

Jika Pemutus suka dua ide bertentangan (misal: Ide A aplikasi seluler, Ide B situs web), jangan kompromi. Kompromi menghasilkan produk buruk.

Sebaliknya, lakukan "Rumble" (Pertarungan). Bangun KEDUANYA. Anda akan menguji Prototipe A lawan Prototipe B di hari Jumat. Biarkan ide terbaik menang di dunia nyata.

The Storyboard Storyboard

In the afternoon, you draw a comic strip on the whiteboard. This is the blueprint for Thursday. It covers every click, every screen, and every text the user will see. No new ideas allowed here; just planning the build.

Sore harinya, Anda menggambar komik strip di papan tulis. Ini cetak biru untuk Kamis. Ini mencakup setiap klik, layar, dan teks yang akan dilihat pengguna. Ide baru dilarang di sini; hanya merencanakan pembuatan.

Case Study: Savioke Robot Studi Kasus: Robot Savioke

Savioke built a robot to deliver items in hotels. They worried: Will guests be scared of it?

The Prototype: They didn't reprogram the robot. They used an old robot, put a screen on it, and controlled it with a joystick (Wizard of Oz style). They focused on the *face* and the *sound* (beeps and boops).

The Result: They learned guests weren't scared; they were charmed. They didn't need complex AI navigation yet; they just needed a friendly personality.

Savioke bikin robot pengantar barang di hotel. Mereka cemas: Apa tamu bakal takut?

Prototipe: Mereka tidak memprogram ulang. Mereka pakai robot tua, pasang layar, dan mengendalikannya dengan joystick (Gaya Wizard of Oz). Mereka fokus pada *wajah* dan *suara* (bip dan bop).

Hasil: Mereka belajar tamu tidak takut; mereka terpesona. Mereka belum butuh navigasi AI rumit; cuma butuh kepribadian ramah.

Friday

The Moment of Truth Momen Kebenaran

The Magic Number: 5 Users

Angka Ajaib: 5 Pengguna

You don't need 100 people. Jakob Nielsen's research shows that 85% of usability problems are discovered by the first 5 people. After 5, the results repeat and you get diminishing returns.

Anda tidak butuh 100 orang. Riset Jakob Nielsen menunjukkan bahwa 85% masalah kegunaan ditemukan oleh 5 orang pertama. Setelah 5, hasilnya berulang dan manfaatnya berkurang.

The Interview Setup

Pengaturan Wawancara

  • Two Rooms: The "Interview Room" (Interviewer + User) and the "Sprint Room" (The rest of the team watching via video link). Dua Ruangan: "Ruang Wawancara" (Pewawancara + Pengguna) dan "Ruang Sprint" (Sisa tim menonton via video).
  • The Interviewer: Be a friendly host. Don't teach them how to use it. Ask open questions: "What is this? What would you do next? What are you thinking right now?" Pewawancara: Jadilah tuan rumah ramah. Jangan ajari mereka cara pakainya. Tanya pertanyaan terbuka: "Ini apa? Apa yang akan Anda lakukan selanjutnya? Apa yang Anda pikirkan sekarang?"
  • The Watchers: The team takes notes silently. Look for Patterns. If 3 out of 5 people struggle with a button, the button is broken. If 3 out of 5 love the pricing, you have a winner. Penonton: Tim mencatat dalam diam. Cari Pola. Jika 3 dari 5 orang kesulitan dengan tombol, tombolnya rusak. Jika 3 dari 5 suka harganya, Anda menang.
