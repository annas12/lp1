The Philosophy Filosofi

Thinking Like an Owner Berpikir Seperti Pemilik

"We are not stock pickers; we are business pickers." "Kami bukan pemilih saham; kami adalah pemilih bisnis."

This book is a collection of Warren Buffett's annual letters to Berkshire Hathaway shareholders. Unlike typical dry corporate reports, Buffett uses these letters to educate. He rejects the short-termism of Wall Street and preaches a philosophy of long-term ownership. He treats shareholders not as faceless investors, but as partners.

Buku ini adalah kumpulan surat tahunan Warren Buffett kepada pemegang saham Berkshire Hathaway. Tidak seperti laporan perusahaan yang kering dan membosankan, Buffett menggunakan surat-surat ini untuk mengedukasi. Dia menolak pandangan jangka pendek Wall Street dan mengkhotbahkan filosofi kepemilikan jangka panjang. Dia memperlakukan pemegang saham bukan sebagai investor tanpa wajah, tetapi sebagai mitra.

The "Partnership" Concept

Buffett begins by stating: "Although our form is corporate, our attitude is partnership." He pledges to treat your money exactly as he would want his money treated if roles were reversed. This implies extreme transparency, admitting mistakes, and never risking the firm's reputation for a quick profit.

Buffett memulai dengan menyatakan: "Meskipun bentuk kami adalah korporasi, sikap kami adalah kemitraan." Dia berjanji untuk memperlakukan uang Anda persis seperti dia ingin uangnya diperlakukan jika peran dibalik. Ini menyiratkan transparansi ekstrem, mengakui kesalahan, dan tidak pernah mempertaruhkan reputasi perusahaan demi keuntungan cepat.

Part 1

Corporate Governance Tata Kelola Perusahaan

Buffett is critical of modern corporate boards. He argues that most directors are "Cocker Spaniels"‚Äîthey just want to please the CEO and keep their high-paying fees.

Buffett sangat kritis terhadap dewan direksi modern. Dia berpendapat bahwa kebanyakan direktur adalah "Anjing Cocker Spaniel"‚Äîmereka hanya ingin menyenangkan CEO dan mempertahankan bayaran tinggi mereka.

Eat Your Own Cooking

Makan Masakan Sendiri

Buffett insists that directors should have true "skin in the game." They should buy shares with their own money, not just receive them as grants. When directors have their own net worth at risk, they stop making reckless acquisitions and start thinking about long-term value preservation.

Stock Options Criticism: Buffett famously dislikes stock options for management. Options have an upside but no downside (a "free lottery ticket"). If the stock goes up, they win. If it goes down, they lose nothing. True ownership requires sharing the downside risk.

Buffett bersikeras bahwa direktur harus memiliki "pertaruhan nyata" (skin in the game). Mereka harus membeli saham dengan uang sendiri, bukan hanya menerimanya sebagai hibah. Ketika kekayaan bersih direktur dipertaruhkan, mereka berhenti melakukan akuisisi sembrono dan mulai memikirkan pelestarian nilai jangka panjang.

Kritik Opsi Saham: Buffett terkenal tidak menyukai opsi saham untuk manajemen. Opsi memiliki sisi positif tapi tanpa sisi negatif ("tiket lotere gratis"). Jika saham naik, mereka menang. Jika turun, mereka tidak rugi apa-apa. Kepemilikan sejati mengharuskan berbagi risiko kerugian.

Part 2

Finance & Investing Keuangan & Investasi

Buffett borrows heavily from his mentor, Ben Graham. The core distinction is between Price (what you pay) and Value (what you get).

Buffett meminjam banyak dari mentornya, Ben Graham. Perbedaan intinya adalah antara Harga (apa yang Anda bayar) dan Nilai (apa yang Anda dapatkan).

The Parable of Mr. Market

Perumpamaan Mr. Market

Imagine you are in business with a partner named Mr. Market. Every day, he offers to buy your share or sell you his share. However, Mr. Market is manic-depressive.

Some days, he is euphoric and offers a very high price. Other days, he is depressed and offers a very low price.

The Lesson: The market is there to serve you, not to instruct you. You should be happy when he is depressed (so you can buy cheap) and cautious when he is euphoric (so you can sell). Do not let his moods dictate your valuation of the business.

Bayangkan Anda berbisnis dengan mitra bernama Mr. Market. Setiap hari, dia menawarkan untuk membeli saham Anda atau menjual sahamnya kepada Anda. Namun, Mr. Market mengidap manik-depresif.

Kadang, dia euforia dan menawar harga sangat tinggi. Di hari lain, dia depresi dan menawar harga sangat rendah.

Pelajaran: Pasar ada untuk melayani Anda, bukan untuk menginstruksikan Anda. Anda harus senang saat dia depresi (agar bisa beli murah) dan waspada saat dia euforia (agar bisa jual). Jangan biarkan suasana hatinya mendikte penilaian Anda terhadap bisnis.

Circle of Competence Lingkaran Kompetensi

You don't need to be an expert on every company. You just need to be able to evaluate companies within your "Circle of Competence." The size of the circle is not important; knowing its boundaries is vital. If you don't understand tech, don't invest in tech just because others are doing it. Stick to what you know (e.g., Chewing Gum, Bricks, Insurance).

Anda tidak perlu jadi ahli di setiap perusahaan. Anda hanya perlu mampu mengevaluasi perusahaan dalam "Lingkaran Kompetensi" Anda. Ukuran lingkaran tidak penting; mengetahui batas-batasnya itu vital. Jika Anda tidak paham teknologi, jangan investasi di teknologi hanya karena orang lain melakukannya. Tetap pada apa yang Anda tahu (misal: Permen Karet, Batu Bata, Asuransi).

Part 3

Alternatives to Common Stock Alternatif Saham Biasa

The Danger of Junk Bonds

Bahaya Obligasi Sampah

Buffett warns against high-yield "Junk Bonds." He argues that defaults are not random; they are correlated. In a recession, many junk bonds fail at once.

He famously called derivatives "Financial Weapons of Mass Destruction." They create a web of risk where if one institution falls, it drags everyone else down (Systemic Risk). He avoids complex financial instruments he cannot fully understand or value.

Buffett memperingatkan tentang "Obligasi Sampah" (Junk Bonds) berimbal hasil tinggi. Dia berargumen bahwa gagal bayar itu tidak acak; mereka berkorelasi. Dalam resesi, banyak obligasi sampah gagal bersamaan.

Dia terkenal menyebut derivatif sebagai "Senjata Pemusnah Massal Finansial." Mereka menciptakan jaring risiko di mana jika satu institusi jatuh, ia menyeret yang lain (Risiko Sistemik). Dia menghindari instrumen keuangan rumit yang tidak bisa dia pahami atau nilai sepenuhnya.

Part 4

Accounting Shenanigans Kecurangan Akuntansi

Buffett despises "Creative Accounting." He believes standard GAAP accounting often hides the true economic reality of a business.

Buffett membenci "Akuntansi Kreatif." Dia percaya standar akuntansi GAAP sering menyembunyikan realitas ekonomi bisnis yang sebenarnya.

Why EBITDA is a Lie Kenapa EBITDA itu Bohong

EBITDA stands for Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.

Buffett hates it because it ignores Depreciation. He asks: "Does management think the Tooth Fairy pays for capital expenditures?" Machines wear out and need replacement. That is a real cost. Ignoring it inflates earnings artificially.

EBITDA adalah Pendapatan Sebelum Bunga, Pajak, Depresiasi, dan Amortisasi.

Buffett membencinya karena mengabaikan Depresiasi. Dia bertanya: "Apa manajemen pikir Peri Gigi yang bayar belanja modal?" Mesin aus dan perlu diganti. Itu biaya nyata. Mengabaikannya menggelembungkan pendapatan secara artifisial.

Owner Earnings Laba Pemilik (Owner Earnings)

This is Buffett's preferred metric.

Formula: Reported Earnings + Depreciation/Amortization - Capital Expenditures (needed to maintain position).

This tells you the actual cash the business generates that can be taken out by the owner without hurting the business.

Ini metrik pilihan Buffett.

Rumus: Pendapatan Dilaporkan + Depresiasi/Amortisasi - Belanja Modal (yang dibutuhkan untuk mempertahankan posisi).

Ini memberi tahu Anda uang tunai aktual yang dihasilkan bisnis yang bisa diambil pemilik tanpa merusak bisnis.

Part 5

Legendary Case Studies Studi Kasus Legendaris

Case Study: See's Candies (Pricing Power)

Studi Kasus: See's Candies (Kekuatan Harga)

Berkshire bought See's Candies in 1972 for $25 million. It was a turning point for Buffett away from "cheap" stocks to "quality" stocks.

The Lesson: See's had an "Economic Moat"‚ÄîBrand Loyalty. People gave See's boxes as gifts. They wouldn't buy a cheaper brand because if the chocolate was bad, the gift failed.

This allowed See's to raise prices every year (Pricing Power) without losing customers. This requires very little new capital, making it a cash-generating machine.

Berkshire membeli See's Candies tahun 1972 seharga $25 juta. Ini titik balik Buffett dari saham "murah" ke saham "berkualitas".

Pelajaran: See's punya "Parit Ekonomi"‚ÄîLoyalitas Merek. Orang memberi kotak See's sebagai hadiah. Mereka tidak mau beli merek murah karena jika cokelatnya tidak enak, hadiahnya gagal.

Ini memungkinkan See's menaikkan harga tiap tahun (Kekuatan Harga) tanpa kehilangan pelanggan. Ini butuh sedikit modal baru, menjadikannya mesin pencetak uang.

Case Study: GEICO & The Float Studi Kasus: GEICO & Float

Buffett loves insurance business because of "Float."

Customers pay premiums upfront (money comes in). Claims are paid later (money goes out). In the meantime, Berkshire holds billions of dollars of "other people's money" for free.

Unlike a bank loan which charges interest, Float is often cost-free (if the insurance underwriting is disciplined). Buffett uses this massive pool of free money to invest in stocks, supercharging returns.

Buffett suka bisnis asuransi karena "Float" (Dana Mengambang).

Pelanggan bayar premi di muka (uang masuk). Klaim dibayar nanti (uang keluar). Sementara itu, Berkshire memegang miliaran dolar "uang orang lain" secara gratis.

Beda dengan pinjaman bank yang kena bunga, Float seringkali bebas biaya (jika penjaminan asuransinya disiplin). Buffett menggunakan kolam uang gratis yang masif ini untuk investasi saham, melipatgandakan keuntungan.
