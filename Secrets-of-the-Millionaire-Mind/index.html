The 3 Sources of Programming

3 Sumber Pemrograman

  • Verbal Programming: What did you hear as a child? ("Money is the root of all evil," "We can't afford it"). These phrases become your subconscious reality. Pemrograman Verbal: Apa yang Anda dengar saat kecil? ("Uang akar segala kejahatan," "Kita tak mampu membelinya"). Frasa ini jadi realitas bawah sadar Anda.
  • Modeling: What did you see? Were your parents spenders or savers? Did they fight about money? You likely mimic one of your parents or rebel against them. Pemodelan: Apa yang Anda lihat? Orang tua Anda boros atau hemat? Apa mereka bertengkar soal uang? Anda kemungkinan meniru salah satu atau memberontak melawannya.
  • Specific Incidents: Emotional experiences related to money. (e.g., Seeing your mom cry because she couldn't buy dinner). These create trauma-based financial habits. Insiden Spesifik: Pengalaman emosional terkait uang. (misal: Melihat ibu menangis karena tak bisa beli makan malam). Ini menciptakan kebiasaan finansial berbasis trauma.

Wealth File #1 Berkas Kekayaan #1

This is the most fundamental difference between rich and poor thinking.

Ini adalah perbedaan paling mendasar antara pemikiran kaya dan miskin.

Rich People Believe: Orang Kaya Percaya:
"I Create My Life"
"Saya Menciptakan Hidup Saya"

They take 100% responsibility. If they are broke, they admit it's their fault. Because they are responsible, they have the power to change it.

Mereka ambil tanggung jawab 100%. Jika mereka bangkrut, mereka akui itu salah mereka. Karena bertanggung jawab, mereka punya kekuatan untuk mengubahnya.

Poor People Believe: Orang Miskin Percaya:
"Life Happens to Me"
"Hidup Terjadi Pada Saya"

They play the role of the victim. Eker identifies 3 clues of a victim:
1. Blame: (The economy, the boss, the spouse).
2. Justify: ("Money isn't important anyway").
3. Complain: complaining is a "crap magnet." What you focus on expands.

Mereka memainkan peran korban. Eker mengidentifikasi 3 ciri korban:
1. Menyalahkan: (Ekonomi, bos, pasangan).
2. Membenarkan: ("Uang tidak penting kok").
3. Mengeluh: Mengeluh adalah "magnet kotoran." Apa yang Anda fokuskan akan membesar.

Part 3

Key Wealth Files Berkas Kekayaan Kunci

Eker lists 17 Wealth Files. Here are two of the most impactful ones regarding attitude and ambition.

Eker mencantumkan 17 Berkas Kekayaan. Berikut adalah dua yang paling berdampak mengenai sikap dan ambisi.

File #6: Admire vs. Resent

Berkas #6: Mengagumi vs. Membenci

Rich people admire other rich and successful people. Poor people resent them.

It is impossible to become something you despise. If you think rich people are "greedy crooks," you will never become rich, because you don't want to be a greedy crook.

Huna Wisdom: "Bless that which you want." If you see a beautiful house, bless the house and bless the owner. Don't throw a stone at it.

Orang kaya mengagumi orang kaya dan sukses lainnya. Orang miskin membenci mereka.

Mustahil menjadi sesuatu yang Anda benci. Jika Anda pikir orang kaya itu "penipu serakah," Anda takkan pernah kaya, karena Anda tidak mau jadi penipu serakah.

Bijak Huna: "Berkati apa yang Anda inginkan." Jika lihat rumah indah, berkati rumahnya dan pemiliknya. Jangan lempar batu.

File #4: Think Big

Berkas #4: Berpikir Besar

Rich people think big. Poor people think small.

The Law of Income states: "You will be paid in direct proportion to the value you deliver according to the marketplace."
Thinking small (playing it safe) leads to being broke and miserable. Thinking big leads to influence and wealth. Ask yourself: "How can I solve this problem for 1,000 people instead of 1?"

Orang kaya berpikir besar. Orang miskin berpikir kecil.

Hukum Pendapatan menyatakan: "Anda akan dibayar berbanding lurus dengan nilai yang Anda berikan menurut pasar."
Berpikir kecil (main aman) mengarah pada kebangkrutan dan kesengsaraan. Berpikir besar mengarah pada pengaruh dan kekayaan. Tanya diri sendiri: "Gimana saya bisa memecahkan masalah ini untuk 1.000 orang, bukan cuma 1?"
