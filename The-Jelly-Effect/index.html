The Jelly Effect

How to Make Your Communication Stick Bagaimana Membuat Komunikasi Anda Menempel

The Problem Masalahnya

Stop Throwing Jelly Berhenti Melempar Jeli

"Most communication is like filling a bucket with jelly and throwing it at a wall. It makes a mess, and very little of it sticks." "Kebanyakan komunikasi itu seperti mengisi ember dengan jeli dan melemparnya ke dinding. Itu bikin berantakan, dan sangat sedikit yang menempel."

We overload people with information. We talk about ourselves, our history, and our features. But audiences (clients, colleagues, bosses) are busy and bored. They don't care about the "Jelly" (excess info). They only care about what's in it for them.

Kita membebani orang dengan informasi. Kita bicara tentang diri kita, sejarah kita, dan fitur kita. Tapi audiens (klien, rekan, bos) itu sibuk dan bosan. Mereka tidak peduli dengan "Jeli" (info berlebih). Mereka hanya peduli apa untungnya bagi mereka.

The "So What?" Filter

Filter "Lalu Kenapa?"

To remove the jelly, imagine your audience shouting "So What?" after every sentence you speak.

You: "We were founded in 1995."
Audience: "So what?"
You: "So we have deep experience."
Audience: "So what?"
You: "So we won't make rookie mistakes with your account." (Ah! Now that sticks).

Untuk membuang jeli, bayangkan audiens Anda berteriak "Lalu Kenapa?" setelah setiap kalimat Anda.

Anda: "Kami didirikan tahun 1995."
Audiens: "Lalu kenapa?"
Anda: "Jadi kami punya pengalaman mendalam."
Audiens: "Lalu kenapa?"
Anda: "Jadi kami tidak akan melakukan kesalahan pemula dengan akun Anda." (Nah! Baru itu menempel).

Part 1

The AFTERs Concept Konsep "AFTERs"

This is the most critical concept in the book. Most people communicate the "During" (the process). Great communicators communicate the "After" (the result).

Ini adalah konsep paling kritis dalam buku ini. Kebanyakan orang mengkomunikasikan "Saat" (prosesnya). Komunikator hebat mengkomunikasikan "Setelah" (hasilnya).

‚ùå Focusing on "During" ‚ùå Fokus pada "Saat" (Proses)

"I am a web designer. I write code, design layouts, and optimize images."

Problem: This is boring. It sounds like hard work. The client doesn't care about code.

"Saya desainer web. Saya nulis kode, desain tata letak, dan optimasi gambar."

Masalah: Ini membosankan. Terdengar seperti kerja keras. Klien tidak peduli soal kode.

‚úÖ Focusing on "AFTERs" ‚úÖ Fokus pada "Setelah" (Hasil)

"I help businesses get more customers online. After working with me, your website will generate twice as many leads."

Benefit: This is compelling. The client buys the future state, not the service.

"Saya bantu bisnis dapat lebih banyak pelanggan online. Setelah kerja dengan saya, web Anda akan hasilkan lead dua kali lipat."

Manfaat: Ini menarik. Klien membeli keadaan masa depan, bukan layanannya.

Technique: Future-Based Questions Teknik: Pertanyaan Berbasis Masa Depan

To find out what a client wants, don't ask "What do you want?" Ask: "Imagine it's 6 months from now and this project was a huge success. What has happened after we finished that makes you so happy?".

Untuk tahu apa yang klien mau, jangan tanya "Anda mau apa?". Tanya: "Bayangkan 6 bulan dari sekarang dan proyek ini sukses besar. Apa yang terjadi setelah kita selesai yang bikin Anda begitu senang?".

Part 2

Networking Re-defined Definisi Ulang Networking

Most people hate networking because they think it's about "selling to strangers." Andy Bounds redefines it completely.

Kebanyakan orang benci networking karena mereka pikir itu soal "jualan ke orang asing." Andy Bounds mendefinisikan ulang sepenuhnya.

The New Definition

Definisi Baru

"Networking is simply helping people achieve their AFTERs."

Don't go to a room to sell. Go to a room to help. When you ask someone, "What are you working on right now?" and they tell you a problem, your goal is to help them solve it‚Äîeven if it has nothing to do with your product.

"Networking hanyalah membantu orang mencapai AFTERs (tujuan akhir) mereka."

Jangan masuk ruangan untuk jualan. Masuklah untuk membantu. Saat Anda tanya, "Lagi ngerjain apa sekarang?" dan mereka cerita masalah, tujuan Anda adalah bantu menyelesaikannya‚Äîmeski gak ada hubungannya dengan produk Anda.

Example: If someone needs a plumber, and you are a web designer, connect them with a good plumber. You become a trusted connector. They will remember you when they (or their friends) need a website. Contoh: Jika seseorang butuh tukang ledeng, dan Anda desainer web, hubungkan mereka dengan tukang ledeng bagus. Anda jadi penghubung terpercaya. Mereka akan ingat Anda saat mereka (atau teman mereka) butuh web.
The "What do you do?" Trap Perangkap "Kerja Apa?"

Never answer with your job title ("I'm an accountant"). That's Jelly.
Answer with the AFTERs: "You know how small businesses struggle to pay tax on time? I ensure they never pay a fine again."
This sparks curiosity ("Really? How?").

Jangan pernah jawab dengan jabatan ("Saya akuntan"). Itu Jeli.
Jawab dengan AFTERs: "Tahu kan bisnis kecil susah bayar pajak tepat waktu? Saya pastikan mereka tidak pernah kena denda lagi."
Ini memicu rasa ingin tahu ("Oya? Gimana caranya?").

Part 4

Presenting with RAP Presentasi dengan RAP

Stop spending 90% of your time on slides. Use the RAP method to prepare presentations that actually convert.

Berhenti habiskan 90% waktu Anda untuk slide. Gunakan metode RAP untuk menyiapkan presentasi yang benar-benar mengonversi.

  • R = Results (The AFTERs) Start by defining exactly what you want the audience to DO after the presentation. If you don't know, don't present. Mulai dengan mendefinisikan persis apa yang Anda ingin audiens LAKUKAN setelah presentasi. Jika tidak tahu, jangan presentasi.
  • A = Audience Who are they? What are their problems? What questions will they ask? Tailor the content to their AFTERs, not your ego. Siapa mereka? Apa masalah mereka? Pertanyaan apa yang akan mereka ajukan? Sesuaikan konten dengan AFTERs mereka, bukan ego Anda.
  • P = Preparation Divide your prep time:

Pro-Tip: Slide Titles Tips Pro: Judul Slide

Never use titles like "Agenda" or "Market Overview." Use benefit-driven titles. Change "Market Overview" to "Why this market is ripe for your growth." Put the AFTERs in the headline.

1. Bank Besar (Menang 18/18)

A major bank was losing pitches. They typically started their presentations with 10 slides about their history, their offices, and their awards (Pure Jelly).

The Change: Andy helped them flip it. They started Slide 1 with the client's problem and the specific AFTERs (e.g., "How you can save ¬£20 million"). They moved their company history to the Appendix (or bin).

2. Barclays (Kesepakatan ¬£2,6 Miliar)
