Core Philosophy Filosofi Inti

Cashflow is King Arus Kas adalah Raja

"Turnover is vanity, profit is sanity, but cash is king." "Omzet adalah kesombongan, profit adalah kewarasan, tapi uang tunai adalah raja."

Brad Sugars emphasizes that most business owners work in their business, not on their business. They are technicians suffering from an "entrepreneurial seizure." To fix this, you must treat your business as a profitable enterprise that works without you. The lifeblood of this enterprise is Cashflow.

Brad Sugars menekankan bahwa sebagian besar pemilik bisnis bekerja di dalam bisnis mereka, bukan pada bisnis mereka. Mereka adalah teknisi yang menderita "serangan kewirausahaan." Untuk memperbaikinya, Anda harus memperlakukan bisnis Anda sebagai perusahaan yang menguntungkan yang bekerja tanpa Anda. Darah kehidupan perusahaan ini adalah Arus Kas.

The Cash Gap

Celah Kas (Cash Gap)

Many profitable businesses go bankrupt. Why? Because of the Cash Gap.

If you pay your suppliers on Day 1, but your customers pay you on Day 60, you have a 60-day cash gap. You must fund operations for 60 days from your own pocket.

The Goal: Narrow the gap. Get paid faster (upfront deposits) and pay suppliers slower (negotiate terms).

Banyak bisnis yang untung (di atas kertas) tapi bangkrut. Kenapa? Karena Celah Kas.

Jika Anda membayar pemasok di Hari 1, tapi pelanggan membayar Anda di Hari 60, Anda punya celah kas 60 hari. Anda harus membiayai operasi selama 60 hari dari kantong sendiri.

Tujuannya: Persempit celah itu. Minta bayaran lebih cepat (deposit di muka) dan bayar pemasok lebih lambat (negosiasi termin).

The Power of 10%

Kekuatan 10%

If you increase each of the 5 variables by just 10%, you don't get a 10% increase in profit. You get a massive compound effect.

The Math: 1.1 x 1.1 x 1.1 x 1.1 x 1.1 = 1.61

Result: A 10% increase in the 5 areas leads to a 61% increase in Profits.

Jika Anda meningkatkan masing-masing dari 5 variabel hanya sebesar 10%, Anda tidak mendapatkan kenaikan profit 10%. Anda mendapatkan efek majemuk yang masif.

Part 2

Getting Customers Mendapatkan Pelanggan

1. Number of Leads

1. Jumlah Leads (Prospek)

A lead is anyone who has contacted you or whom you have contacted. Stop thinking about "Advertising"; think about "Buying Customers."

Lead adalah siapa saja yang menghubungi Anda atau yang Anda hubungi. Berhenti berpikir tentang "Iklan"; berpikirlah tentang "Membeli Pelanggan."

Strategy: The USP

Unique Selling Proposition. Why should I buy from you? If you don't have a USP, you compete on price.
Example: Domino's Pizza: "Fresh, hot pizza in 30 minutes or it's free."

Proposisi Penjualan Unik. Kenapa saya harus beli dari Anda? Jika tidak punya USP, Anda bersaing harga.
Contoh: Domino's Pizza: "Pizza segar dan panas dalam 30 menit atau gratis."

Strategy: The Guarantee

Remove the risk. If you are good, a guarantee costs you nothing. If you are bad, fix your product.
Example: "100% money back, no questions asked."

Hapus risikonya. Jika Anda bagus, garansi tidak memakan biaya. Jika Anda buruk, perbaiki produknya.
Contoh: "100% uang kembali, tanpa pertanyaan."

2. Conversion Rate

2. Tingkat Konversi

How many leads actually buy? If 10 people call and 2 buy, your rate is 20%.

Berapa banyak lead yang benar-benar beli? Jika 10 orang telepon dan 2 beli, tingkat Anda 20%.

  • Scripts: Don't let salespeople wing it. Use proven scripts. Skrip: Jangan biarkan penjual ngasal. Gunakan skrip teruji.
  • Benefits, not Features: Don't sell the drill (feature); sell the hole (benefit). Manfaat, bukan Fitur: Jangan jual bornya (fitur); jual lubangnya (manfaat).
Part 3

Maximizing Value Memaksimalkan Nilai

It is 6x cheaper to sell to an existing customer than to find a new one. This section focuses on mining the gold you already have.

6x lebih murah menjual ke pelanggan lama daripada mencari pelanggan baru. Bagian ini fokus pada menggali emas yang sudah Anda miliki.

3. Average Dollar Sale

3. Rata-rata Nilai Penjualan

Add-ons (McDonald's)

"Do you want fries with that?" This question makes McDonald's millions daily.
Strategy: Checklist. "You bought the suit, do you need the tie, the shirt, and the belt?" Never assume they don't want it.

"Mau tambah kentang goreng?" Pertanyaan ini menghasilkan jutaan bagi McDonald's setiap hari.
Strategi: Daftar periksa. "Anda beli jasnya, apa butuh dasi, kemeja, dan ikat pinggangnya?" Jangan berasumsi mereka tidak mau.

Price Increases

Most owners are terrified to raise prices.
Reality: If you raise prices by 10%, you might lose 5% of customers, but your profit usually goes UP because you do less work for more money.

Kebanyakan pemilik takut menaikkan harga.
Realita: Jika Anda naikkan harga 10%, mungkin hilang 5% pelanggan, tapi profit biasanya NAIK karena kerja lebih sedikit untuk uang lebih banyak.

4. Number of Transactions

4. Jumlah Transaksi

How often do they come back? Once a year? Twice?

Strategy: Database Marketing. You must keep a database of all customers (Email/Phone). Send them offers, newsletters, and birthday cards.
"Out of sight, out of mind." If you don't contact them, they forget you.

Seberapa sering mereka kembali? Setahun sekali? Dua kali?

Strategi: Pemasaran Database. Anda harus punya database semua pelanggan (Email/HP). Kirim penawaran, buletin, kartu ulang tahun.
"Jauh di mata, jauh di hati." Jika Anda tidak menghubungi mereka, mereka lupa Anda.

Part 4

Margins Margin (Keuntungan Bersih)

5. Profit Margins

5. Margin Keuntungan

Revenue is vanity. Margin is sanity. If you sell $1M but it costs $999k to make, you are broke.

Omzet adalah kesombongan. Margin adalah kewarasan. Jika Anda jual $1 Juta tapi biayanya $999rb, Anda bangkrut.

  • No Discounts: Discounting kills margins fast. Instead of 10% off, give a "Value Add" (e.g., Free Bonus Gift). It has high perceived value but low cost to you. Jangan Diskon: Diskon membunuh margin dengan cepat. Daripada diskon 10%, beri "Nilai Tambah" (misal: Hadiah Bonus). Nilainya terlihat tinggi tapi biayanya rendah buat Anda.
  • Fire D-Grade Customers: The customers who complain the most usually pay the least. Fire them. It frees up time to serve the A-Grade customers who pay full price. Pecat Pelanggan Kelas D: Pelanggan yang paling banyak komplain biasanya bayar paling sedikit. Pecat mereka. Ini membebaskan waktu untuk melayani pelanggan Kelas A yang bayar penuh.
Part 5

Real World Application Penerapan Dunia Nyata

Case Study: The Hairdresser

Studi Kasus: Penata Rambut

Problem: Working 60 hours/week, barely breaking even.

Action 1 (Avg Sale): She started suggesting premium shampoos to every client.
Action 2 (Transactions): She started booking the next appointment before the client left the salon (increasing frequency from 8 weeks to 6 weeks).
Action 3 (Leads): She created a referral program: "Bring a friend, you get a free treatment."

Result: Revenue doubled in 6 months without working extra hours.

Masalah: Kerja 60 jam/minggu, pas-pasan.

Aksi 1 (Rata2 Jual): Dia mulai menyarankan sampo premium ke setiap klien.
Aksi 2 (Transaksi): Dia mulai menjadwalkan janji temu berikutnya sebelum klien keluar salon (meningkatkan frekuensi dari 8 minggu jadi 6 minggu).
Aksi 3 (Leads): Dia buat program referensi: "Bawa teman, dapat perawatan gratis."

Hasil: Omzet naik dua kali lipat dalam 6 bulan tanpa tambah jam kerja.

Case Study: The Retail Store (Bundling)

Studi Kasus: Toko Ritel (Bundling)

Problem: Competition from big box stores on price.

Action (Margin/Avg Sale): Instead of discounting individual items, the owner created "Packages."
Instead of selling a camera for $500 (low margin), he sold a "Photographer Starter Kit" (Camera + Bag + Tripod + Memory Card + Cleaning Cloth) for $650.

Result: Competitors couldn't compare prices directly because the bundle was unique. The margin on accessories (Bag/Tripod) was huge (70%), masking the low margin on the camera. Profits soared.

Masalah: Persaingan harga dengan toko raksasa.

Aksi (Margin/Rata2 Jual): Daripada diskon barang satuan, pemilik membuat "Paket."
Daripada jual kamera $500 (margin rendah), dia jual "Paket Pemula Fotografer" (Kamera + Tas + Tripod + Kartu Memori + Kain Lap) seharga $650.

Hasil: Pesaing tidak bisa membandingkan harga langsung karena paketnya unik. Margin aksesoris (Tas/Tripod) sangat besar (70%), menutupi margin rendah kamera. Profit melonjak.
